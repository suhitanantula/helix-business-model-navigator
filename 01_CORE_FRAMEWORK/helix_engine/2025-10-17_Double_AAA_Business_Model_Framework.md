## The Breakthrough Insight

### Traditional MIT CISR Framework (2025)

MIT CISR identified two axes:
- **Vertical**: Action on Behalf of Customers (Assist vs. Represent)
- **Horizontal**: Business Execution (Structured vs. Adaptive)

This created 4 business models: Existing+, Customer Proxy, Modular Curator, Orchestrator.

### The Double-AAA Reframe

**Both axes are actually AAA progressions:**

**Vertical Axis: AAA on Customer Action**
- **Assist** Customers â†’ Help them achieve outcomes (supportive role)
- **Augment** Customers â†’ Co-create outcomes with them (partnership)
- **Adapt** For Customers â†’ Autonomously achieve outcomes on their behalf (representation)

**Horizontal Axis: AAA on Business Execution**
- **Assist** Execution â†’ Structured, predefined processes (Lines-dominant, humans in loop + at helm)
- **Augment** Execution â†’ Hybrid, adaptive processes (Loops-dominant, learning systems)
- **Adapt** Execution â†’ Autonomous, AI-orchestrated (Vibes-dominant, dynamic orchestration)

## The Nine Business Model States

### TIER 1: ASSIST CUSTOMER (Support Role)

#### **Q1: Basic Assistant** (Assist/Assist)
**Description:** AI helps customers through predefined, structured processes

**Characteristics:**
- **Customer Role:** Self-service with AI guidance
- **Execution:** Rule-based, scripted workflows
- **AI Capability:** Pattern matching, retrieval, simple automation
- **Revenue Model:** Traditional product/service sales
- **LLV Pattern:** Lines-Lines (pure structure on both axes)
- **Human Role:** In the loop for all decisions, at the helm for strategy

**Examples:**
- FAQ chatbot with predefined responses
- Form-filling assistant
- Basic scheduling tool
- Simple product recommendation engine

**Organisational Requirements:**
- Data: Structured, siloed datasets
- Systems: Basic APIs optional
- Human-AI: Prompt literacy, output reviewing
- Governance: Usage policy, basic boundaries
- Inclusion: Basic accessibility compliance
- Org Readiness: Pilot funding, trust building

---

#### **Q2: Smart Assistant** (Assist/Augment)
**Description:** AI helps customers with adaptive, learning-enhanced processes

**Characteristics:**
- **Customer Role:** Guided by context-aware AI suggestions
- **Execution:** AI adapts recommendations based on feedback loops
- **AI Capability:** Machine learning, personalisation, continuous improvement
- **Revenue Model:** Enhanced product/service value, premium tiers
- **LLV Pattern:** Lines-Loops (structured intent, adaptive delivery)
- **Human Role:** In the loop with escalation paths, at the helm for boundaries

**Examples:**
- Customer service AI that learns from interactions
- Personalised shopping assistant
- Adaptive learning platform
- Context-aware help system

**Organisational Requirements:**
- Data: Integrated across key touchpoints
- Systems: API layer for data sharing
- Human-AI: Feedback provision, pattern recognition
- Governance: Escalation protocols, bias monitoring
- Inclusion: Multi-modal access, language support
- Org Readiness: Change management, success metrics

---

#### **Q3: Intelligent Assistant** (Assist/Adapt)
**Description:** AI proactively helps customers through autonomous orchestration

**Characteristics:**
- **Customer Role:** Receives proactive, highly personalised assistance
- **Execution:** AI autonomously orchestrates multiple systems
- **AI Capability:** Multi-agent systems, predictive analytics, real-time optimisation
- **Revenue Model:** Premium assistance services, outcome-based pricing
- **LLV Pattern:** Lines-Vibes (structured customer intent, dynamic system delivery)
- **Human Role:** At the helm only, override capability, strategic oversight

**Examples:**
- Proactive customer service (predicting and solving issues)
- Intelligent inventory management for customers
- Adaptive security monitoring
- Predictive maintenance alerts

**Organisational Requirements:**
- Data: Real-time, cross-system integration
- Systems: Microservices, event-driven architecture
- Human-AI: Override judgment, systemic monitoring
- Governance: Active review panels, incident reporting
- Inclusion: Universal design principles
- Org Readiness: Cultural readiness for autonomy

## Strategic Insights

### 1. MIT CISR Mapped to the Double-AAA Framework

**Original MIT CISR Four Models:**
- **Existing+** â†’ Q1-Q2 (Basic/Smart Assistant)
- **Customer Proxy** â†’ Q7 (Traditional Proxy)

**What We've Revealed:**
- **5 additional strategic positions** MIT didn't identify
- **Clear progression pathways** between models
- **Granular understanding** of capability requirements

### 2. Multiple Pathways to Transformation

Organisations don't have to move linearly. Different strategic pathways:

**Horizontal Progression (Execution First):**
Q1 â†’ Q2 â†’ Q3: Build execution capability before customer autonomy
- Best for: Risk-averse industries, regulated sectors
- Example: Banking, healthcare, legal services

**Vertical Progression (Customer First):**
Q1 â†’ Q4 â†’ Q7: Increase customer autonomy with structured processes
- Best for: Trust-critical relationships
- Example: Financial advisory, education

**Diagonal Progression (Balanced):**
Q1 â†’ Q5 â†’ Q9: Balanced advancement on both axes
- Best for: Technology companies, digital natives
- Example: Fintech, healthtech, edtech

**Strategic Skip:**
Q1 â†’ Q5 or Q1 â†’ Q7: Jump to high-value positions
- Risk: Capability gaps, governance failures
- Requires: Significant investment, talent acquisition

### 3. Risk Increases Non-Linearly

Moving diagonally from Q1 â†’ Q9 represents exponential complexity:

**Technical Risk:**
- Q1-Q3: Moderate (proven technologies)
- Q4-Q6: High (emerging capabilities)
- Q7-Q9: Extreme (frontier AI, autonomous systems)

**Ethical Risk:**
- Q1-Q3: Low (human oversight maintained)
- Q4-Q6: Medium (shared decision-making)
- Q7-Q9: High (autonomous action on behalf of others)

**Governance Risk:**
- Q1-Q3: Manageable (standard policies)
- Q4-Q6: Complex (multi-party governance)
- Q7-Q9: Critical (systemic oversight required)

**Trust Risk:**
- Q1-Q3: Building trust
- Q4-Q6: Maintaining trust through transparency
- Q7-Q9: Proving trust through outcomes

### 4. Industry-Specific Trajectories

Different industries will cluster in different regions:

**Conservative Industries (Finance, Healthcare, Legal):**
- Current state: Q1-Q2
- Near-term: Q2-Q4
- Long-term: Q5-Q7
- Unlikely to reach Q9 soon due to regulation

**Technology Companies:**
- Current state: Q2-Q3
- Near-term: Q5-Q6
- Long-term: Q8-Q9
- Racing to autonomous orchestration

**Manufacturing & Industrial:**
- Current state: Q1
- Near-term: Q2-Q3
- Long-term: Q3-Q6
- Focus on execution autonomy over customer autonomy

**Professional Services:**
- Current state: Q1-Q4
- Near-term: Q4-Q5
- Long-term: Q5-Q6
- Stay in co-creation zone (Augment customer)
