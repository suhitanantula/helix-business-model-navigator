## üìç CURRENT STATE MAPPING: EXISTING+ (Q1-Q2)

### MIT CISR Observation:
*"Most of the company's initiatives to date reside in the Existing+ business model quadrant, with One NZ assisting customers in achieving outcomes through process-focused AI support."*

### Double-AAA Framework Positioning:

**Primary Position: Q1-Q2 (Basic/Smart Assistant)**
- **Customer Action:** Assist (supportive role)
- **Execution:** Assist ‚Üí Augment (structured processes with learning)
- **LLV Signature:** Lines-Lines ‚Üí Lines-Loops

### Specific Initiatives Mapped to Q1-Q2

#### **Q1: Basic Assistant - Knowledge Agents**
**MIT CISR Description:**
- "Knowledge agents assist customers with complex products"
- "Enabling over 60 percent resolution of frequently asked questions and other queries from consumer prepaid customers"
- "40 percent of such queries from enterprise customers (still in beta)"

**Double-AAA Analysis:**
- **Customer Role:** Self-service with AI guidance (Assist)
- **Execution:** Predefined FAQ structure with pattern matching (Assist/Structured)
- **LLV Pattern:** Lines-Lines
  - Lines: Structured question-answer pairs
  - Loops: Minimal - tracking resolution rates
  - Vibes: None - rule-based retrieval
- **Human Role:** In the loop (reviews unresolved queries), at the helm (defines knowledge base)

**Maturity Requirements Validated:**
- ‚úÖ Data: Structured knowledge base (siloed FAQ data)
- ‚úÖ Systems: Basic API for query routing
- ‚úÖ Human-AI: Prompt literacy, output reviewing
- ‚úÖ Governance: Usage policy for customer queries
- ‚úÖ Inclusion: Basic accessibility (text-based interface)
- ‚úÖ Org Readiness: Pilot funding achieved (15 use cases at margin hurdle)

**Revenue Model:** Traditional subscription pricing + efficiency gains (reduced support costs)

**Target Position: Q7 (Traditional Proxy)**
- **Customer Action:** Adapt (acting on behalf of customer)

- **LLV Signature:** Vibes-Lines

### Specific Initiatives Mapped to Q7

#### **Service Agents (Autonomous Action with Authorization)**
**MIT CISR Description:**
- "Service agents already handle tasks such as upgrading plans, raising disconnection requests, and initiating support case tickets"
- "These AI agents still have humans in the loop and humans at the helm to ensure responsible use of AI across the process"
- "Looking ahead, these agents will also predict and resolve issues proactively"

**Double-AAA Analysis:**
- **Customer Role:** Delegator who authorizes specific actions (Adapt)
- **Execution:** Predefined workflow for plan changes, disconnections, tickets (Assist/Structured)
- **LLV Pattern:** Vibes-Lines
  - Lines: Structured process for plan upgrades, disconnection workflows
  - Loops: Minimal currently - action logging
  - Vibes: Autonomous execution on customer's behalf within strict parameters
- **Human Role:**
  - Customer: Sets authorization boundaries
  - Employee: In the loop (reviews actions), at the helm (defines guardrails)

**Maturity Requirements Validated:**
- ‚úÖ Data: Customer preferences, service history, authorization records
- ‚úÖ Systems: Workflow automation for service changes
- ‚úÖ Human-AI: Delegation skills (customers), boundary setting
- ‚úÖ Governance: **Clear liability frameworks** (who's accountable when AI upgrades a plan?)
- ‚úÖ Inclusion: Accessible delegation interfaces
- ‚úÖ Org Readiness: Trust in automation (humans still in loop validates this is early Q7)

**Critical Insight:** *"These AI agents still have humans in the loop and humans at the helm"*
- This validates our Q7 framework requirement: Structured proxy with oversight
- Not yet Q8 (which would have learning/adaptation)
- Not yet Q9 (which would have ecosystem orchestration)

**Future Vision (Q7 ‚Üí Q8 Transition):**
- "Predict and resolve issues proactively"
- This represents moving from Q7 (rule-based proxy) ‚Üí Q8 (learning proxy)
- **Transition trigger:** Outcome tracking + performance learning

### Multi-Path Strategy Analysis

**One NZ is NOT following a linear path. They're executing a sophisticated multi-quadrant strategy:**

**Path 1: Horizontal Progression (Q1 ‚Üí Q2)**
- Knowledge agents ‚Üí Marketing AI
- Building execution sophistication first
- Timeline: Active now (2024-2025)

**Path 2: Diagonal Jump (Q2 ‚Üí Q5)**
- Marketing AI ‚Üí Multi-agent crisis response
- Skipping Q3 and Q4 entirely for network optimization
- Timeline: Early pilots (weather event response)

**Path 3: Vertical Jump (Q1 ‚Üí Q7)**
- Knowledge agents ‚Üí Service agents (proxy actions)
- Increasing customer autonomy delegation
- Timeline: Active transition (2024-2025)

**Path 4: Future Vision (Q5 ‚Üí Q9, Q7 ‚Üí Q9)**
- Multi-agent coordination ‚Üí Autonomous orchestration
- Multiple pathways converging on Q9
- Timeline: Future (3-5+ years)

### Why This Multi-Path Approach?

**Strategic Rationale:**
1. **Use Case Maturity:** Different business functions have different readiness levels
2. **Risk Management:** Diversified transformation reduces dependency on single pathway
3. **Learning Transfer:** Insights from Q2 marketing AI inform Q5 network optimization
4. **Competitive Differentiation:** Multiple quadrants = harder to copy

**This validates our framework's core insight:**
> "Organisations don't have to move linearly. Different strategic pathways exist."
