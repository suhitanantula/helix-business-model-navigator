### Detailed 5Ps Methodology

#### **P1: PERCEIVE** (Sense Deeply with Context + Pattern Recognition)

**Purpose:** Understand current state, sense strategic imperative, detect weak signals

**Activities:**
- **Current Quadrant Assessment:** Where are we today? (Double-AAA diagnostic)
- **Strategic Context Scan:** What's changing in our industry? (Wardley evolution, competitive dynamics)
- **Weak Signal Detection:** What are leading competitors doing? (CBA Gen AI launch = signal for ANZ/NAB/Westpac)
- **Regulatory Landscape:** What's changing in compliance requirements? (APRA CPS 230 = forcing function for banking AI governance)
- **Customer Trust Assessment:** What level of AI autonomy will customers accept? (Trust determines advancement ceiling)

**Tools:**
- Double-AAA quadrant diagnostic questionnaire
- Wardley mapping of AI capabilities
- Industry benchmarking (Evident AI Index for banking, etc.)
- Customer trust surveys
- Regulatory horizon scanning

**Output:** Strategic assessment report defining current position, strategic imperative, target quadrant

**Example: CBA's Perceive Phase (2022)**
- **Current State:** Q4 (Co-Creation Assistant) - multiple AI pilots, no systematic deployment
- **Strategic Imperative:** Gen AI breakthrough (ChatGPT launch) creates customer expectation for conversational banking
- **Weak Signals:** US banks investing heavily in Gen AI, customer trust in AI rising
- **Regulatory Context:** APRA CPS 230 coming July 2025 - governance framework needed
- **Decision:** Must advance to Q5 (Dynamic Curator) to defend competitive position

---

#### **P2: PERFORM** (Act with Intention - Human Judgment + AI Scale)

**Purpose:** Execute within current quadrant with excellence while building foundations for next quadrant

**Activities:**
- **Optimize Current Quadrant:** Maximize value from current business model position
- **Build Foundations:** Develop capabilities required for next quadrant
- **Governance First:** Implement governance frameworks BEFORE advancing (CBA learned this)
- **Talent Development:** Reskill workforce for next quadrant roles
- **Infrastructure Investment:** Build data platforms, ML systems, operational processes

**Tools:**
- Five Aspects transformation roadmap (what must change in Humans, Workflows, Jobs, Strategy, Structure)
- ISO 42001 implementation (governance foundation)
- Capability maturity assessment (are we ready to advance?)
- Workforce reskilling programs
- Technology platform buildout

**Output:** Operational excellence in current quadrant + readiness for advancement

**Example: CBA's Perform Phase (2023)**
- **Optimize Q4:** Improved existing AI use cases (fraud detection, customer service chatbots, risk models)
- **Build Q5 Foundations:**
  - Data Platform: Consolidated customer data (single source of truth)
  - Governance: Built AI Ethics Board, implemented APRA CPS 230 frameworks
  - Talent: Hired 1,000+ AI specialists, retrained customer service teams
  - Infrastructure: Launched AI Factory (6-week product development capability)
- **Investment:** $2.3B annually in AI capability building
- **Result:** Ready to advance to Q5 by late 2023

#### **P3: PORTFOLIO** (Build Options Through Parallel Exploration)

**Purpose:** Run experiments in parallel to test next quadrant viability, reduce advancement risk

**Activities:**
- **Parallel Experiments:** Test 5-10 next-quadrant use cases simultaneously
- **Hypothesis Testing:** Which AI capabilities create value? Which fail?
- **Safe-to-Fail Probes:** Small bets in controlled environments (not bet-the-company moves)
- **Customer Feedback Loops:** Do customers accept higher autonomy? Where do they resist?
- **Governance Testing:** Can our governance frameworks handle next quadrant risks?

**Tools:**
- Innovation lab / AI sandbox environments
- A/B testing frameworks
- Customer pilot programs
- Governance dry runs (test ethics review process with hypothetical scenarios)
- Portfolio management dashboards (track experiment success/failure)

**Output:** 5-10 experiments, 2-3 proven next-quadrant capabilities, 3-5 failures with lessons learned

**Example: CBA's Portfolio Phase (2023-2024)**
- **Experiments Run:**
  1. Gen AI chatbot (customer-facing) ‚úÖ Success
  2. Scam detection AI (50% reduction) ‚úÖ Success
  3. Personalized financial advice ‚úÖ Success
  4. Autonomous loan approvals ‚ùå Failed (regulatory constraints)
  5. AI-generated marketing content ‚úÖ Success
  6. Predictive customer intervention (retained $92M deposits) ‚úÖ Success
  7. Agentic workflows (Q6 prototype) üîÑ In testing
  8. Voice banking AI ‚ùå Failed (customer trust issues)
  9. AI-powered investment recommendations ‚úÖ Success
  10. Real-time fraud intervention ‚úÖ Success

- **Success Rate:** 70% (7 of 10 experiments successful)
- **Key Learnings:**
  - Customers trust AI for fraud/scam protection (safety)
  - Customers trust AI for personalized recommendations (convenience)
  - Customers DON'T trust AI for autonomous financial decisions (control)
  - Governance frameworks can handle Q5, need strengthening for Q6

---

#### **P4: PAUSE / PROMOTE** (Curate What Matters - Decide What Becomes Strategic)

**Purpose:** Decide which experiments to promote to strategic initiatives vs. pause/kill

**Activities:**
- **Portfolio Review:** Assess all experiments against success criteria
- **Strategic Fit Assessment:** Which experiments align with target quadrant positioning?
- **Resource Allocation:** Promote successful experiments to full production, kill failures
- **Governance Review:** Do promoted experiments meet risk/ethics/compliance standards?
- **Communication:** Announce strategic moves to organization, customers, regulators

**Tools:**
- Decision criteria matrix (strategic value √ó technical feasibility √ó governance maturity √ó customer acceptance)
- Business case development for promoted experiments
- Risk assessment for each promotion decision
- Change management planning
- Stakeholder communication plans

**Output:** 2-3 experiments promoted to strategic initiatives, 3-5 paused/killed with lessons captured

**Example: CBA's Pause/Promote Phase (Late 2024)**

**PROMOTED TO STRATEGIC (Q5 Advancement):**
1. **Gen AI Chatbot:** Full production launch, 80% of routine inquiries handled
2. **Scam Detection AI:** Enterprise-wide deployment, 50% scam reduction achieved
3. **Personalized Financial Advice:** Integrated into mobile app, customer engagement +35%
4. **Predictive Customer Intervention:** Scaled to all customer segments, $92M deposits retained
5. **AI-Powered Investment Recommendations:** Wealth management rollout

**PAUSED (Not Ready):**
1. **Agentic Workflows (Q6 Prototype):** Governance frameworks need strengthening, regulatory clarity needed - continue R&D but don't deploy to production yet
2. **Voice Banking AI:** Customer trust insufficient, accuracy not production-ready - revisit in 2026

**KILLED (Failed Experiments):**
1. **Autonomous Loan Approvals:** Regulatory prohibition (APRA won't approve Q7 for credit decisions)
2. **Lessons Learned:** Banking has Q6 ceiling for customer-facing decisions, Q7-Q9 only viable for backend operations

**Strategic Decision:** Advance to Q5 (Dynamic Curator) in 2025, build toward Q6 (Adaptive Partner) for 2026-2027, accept Q7-Q9 not viable for customer-facing banking

- KPI dashboards (business outcomes from AI)

- Communities of practice (AI practitioners sharing learnings)

**Output:** Institutionalized capability, repeatable processes, compounding organizational intelligence

**Systematization:**

- **Risk Reduction:** 50% scam reduction, $92M deposits retained, 20,000 alerts/day automated
